How to carve a pumpkin

The jack-o'-lantern is the most iconic symbol of Halloween. Having a glowing jack-o'-lantern on your doorstep is a Halloween classic. It doesn't matter if you do a simple pumpkin with triangular eyes and a smile or go all out and do something more artistic. Every house needs a jack-o-lantern. This article will learn how to carve a jack o'lantern properly.



Anyone who has ever craved a pumpkin will tell you it's a lot harder and messier than you would think. If you wanted to take the easy way out, you could paint or draw a face on your pumpkin instead but, where is the fun in that.

To carve a good jack-o-lantern, you have to find the perfect pumpkin first. To do so, you must look at multiple factors starting with making sure the skin is smooth and has no cracks or splits. Make sure to look well at the stem and the area around it because that will affect how easy it is to cut into. Finally, the pumpkin should be hard with no soft spots on it anywhere to ensure it is not rotten inside.



Clean any dirt from the carving pumpkin by washing it and turning it on its side. Cut a large circle around the stem of the pumpkin. Remove and save the cut-out piece. Put on gloves and use a spoon or your hands to take out all the inner parts of the pumpkin until you have removed all the soft internal bits and thinned down the walls to make the carving process more manageable. Place all the inner parts in the trash. Feel free to save the seeds for roasting.

You can draw your jack-o'-lantern face on paper, then tape it to the front of your pumpkin and poke holes along the lines you want to carve with a fork or pencil. A pumpkin carving kit with a marker, scraper, cutting tools, and pre-made patterns are also sold in stores.



To cut into the pumpkin, use a gentle back and forth motion and take your time. Make sure to cut precisely along the design you drew on the pumpkin. Keep an excellent downward pressure while pulling the knife back and forth. Stick to your pattern until you've finished carving out the complete design.
